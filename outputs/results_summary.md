# Marketing Campaign — A/B Test & Performance Results

Recipients analysed: **40,000**  |  Channels: 4  |  Regions: 5  |  Segments: 4

| Stage | Count | % of contacted |
|---|--:|--:|

| Converted | 893 | 2.23% |

## 2. A/B test — control (A) vs personalised offer (B)

| Group | Recipients | Conversions | Conv. rate | Revenue / recipient |
|---|--:|--:|--:|--:|
| A (control) | 20,057.0 | 377.0 | 1.88% | ₹57 |
| B (treatment) | 19,943.0 | 516.0 | 2.59% | ₹84 |

- **Absolute lift:** +0.71 pp (B − A)
- **Relative lift:** +37.7%
- **95% CI on absolute lift:** [+0.42 pp, +1.00 pp]
- **Two-proportion z-test:** z = 4.79, p = 1.66e-06
- **Chi-square cross-check:** χ² = 22.6, p = 1.97e-06
- **Verdict:** **statistically significant** — roll out B (revenue/recipient +46.9%).

## 6. Key takeaways

- The personalised offer (B) lifted conversion **+37.7%** vs control, significant at p < 0.05 — recommend full roll-out.
- **High-Value** customers convert best (4.5%) and return the most revenue per recipient — prioritise them for cross-sell / up-sell.
- **SMS** is the strongest channel by conversion — shift budget toward it; reduce spend on the weakest channel.
- Dormant customers convert lowest — candidates for a re-engagement / win-back campaign rather than the standard offer.
